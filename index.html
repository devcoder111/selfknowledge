Wheel of Karma

The set of deeds that we perform in our lifetime is called Karma. It is the script that our character plays in the story of life. Everything we say and do is controlled by this algorithm.

Our Karma drives our thoughts, emotions & feelings and it determines how we analyze and prioritize things. These in-turn drive our conclusions, decisions, and actions. The words we speak, the tone of our speech as well as the expressions, gestures, and body language we use while communicating are also driven by our Karma.

Karma is cyclic in nature and works iteratively. It is powered by the difference between our expectations and reality and it stops when we give up expectations. When this cycle stops, we become relaxed and happy. We then start living life.

This cycle starts shortly after birth, when we witness the actions of others around us and we start associating consequences with those actions.

Emotions & Thoughts

Emotions & Thoughts are mechanisms we use to sense situations. They are generated by our value system in response to situations we witness or are involved in. Emotion is our feeling response to the situation while thought is our intellectual response the situation.

The difference between our expectations and the actual consequences of an action is what generates thoughts and emotions. The nature, quality and intensity of the generated thoughts and emotions depend on the nature, quality and magnitude of this difference.

Feelings & Decisions

Emotions are active till the situation that drives them is active. Once the situation ends, the residual emotion that is left is called a feeling. Emotions are tied to the actual situation while feelings are tied to the memory of a situation.

Feelings keep diluting over time and disappear as the memory of the situation disappears. However, if another situation generates the same emotion again, the feeling gets reinforced and is felt with greater intensity.

Our value system shapes our thoughts, emotions and feelings and these in turn drive our expressions. speech, decisions, and actions.

Expression & Action

The physical act of doing something to achieve a specific goal, along with the associated spoken words, the tone of speech as well as expressions, gestures and body language is called an action.

The difference between the expected and actual outcome of any action we participate in or witness is what generates our thoughts and emotions. These in-turn drive our feelings, decisions, speech, tone of speech, facial expressions, body language and our physical actions.

The belief that we have the power to make things better by changing our circumstances is what motivates our actions.

happy state of mind

Happiness is not a feeling. It's a state of mind. When we are happy, we experience one or more of the following feelings.

Peace

Delinking our state of mind from our circumstances removes the emotional volatility associated with changing circumstances and brings about stability of mind. The absence of emotional volatility results in a deep state of relaxation that is unlike anything you might have ever experienced.

completeness

The absence of volatility associated with changing life circumstances brings us closer to our inner self. Coming in touch with that which we had forgotten makes us feel complete again. When we feel complete within ourselves we stop looking for comfort in external things such as our relationships, achievements or possessions.

contentment

contentment is a deep state of satisfaction that is characterized by lack of desire. We reach this state when we stop seeking satisfaction in the outcome of events of our life. The realization that the experiences of our life are out of our control frees us from desire.

security

We become secure when we stop being afraid of adverse life circumstances. Delinking our state of mind from our circumstances results in a state in which the fear of adverse circumstances no longer impacts us and we feel secure all the time.

unhappiness

The state of mind in which we are dissatisfied with or disturbed by the circumstances of our life is called unhappiness.

In this state our circumstances generate constant thoughts, emotions and feelings within us.

When we are unhappy, we have trouble accepting both situations as well as the attitude of people around us. This friction generates negativity that keep us feeling miserable.

what causes unhappiness?

The belief that we have the power to change our circumstances to match our expectations is the root cause of unhappiness. This belief establishes a relationship between our circumstances and our state of mind i.e. we start feeling good when are circumstances are good and bad when our circumstances are bad.

The difference between our expectations and reality (circumstances) is what motivates us to act out our part in the story of life. Since expectations rarely meet reality, we remain unhappy most of our life.

false positive feelings

Confusion things that make us unhappy with those that make up happy is a common mistake. Many people associate the following feelings with happiness. However, we experience these feelings in a state of deep unhappiness.

Pleasure

Pleasure is a mental experience that we find enjoyable, positive, or worth seeking. It is a component of our reward system that drives us to seek things we find pleasurable.

Pleasure is a temporary experience and as a cost associated with it. Constantly seeking things that give us pleasure and inability to get satisfied drive us into further unhappiness.

Achievement

A sense of achievement is a temporary pleasurable feeling we get when we reach our goals. It is a taste of how we feel wen our expectations meet reality. Our sense of achievement keeps us motivated so we can improve upon our achievements. The boost we get from it is temporary and intensifies our struggle for success even more.

Self Confidence

Self-confidence is the trust one has in their abilities. As a person becomes more experienced, their trust in their ability increases. Conversly, lack of success kills self-confidence.

Self-confidence is a state that depends on the circumstances of our life. When we experience a bad stretch, our self-confidence is the first thing that is impacted.

enjoyable relationships

Our relationships have a deep impact on our state of mind. They generate a constant stream of emotions, thoughts and feelings that drive our emotional well-being.

Well balanced relationships make our life enjoyable and fill it with relaxtion and laughter, while friction inour relationships generate constant negativity that keeps us feeling miserable.

A perfect relationship is one that does not generate negativity and requires no effort to sustain.

what is a relationship?

Our Relationships are like accounting ledgers that keep track of the things that we do for others along with what we get back in return. The value of what we give and what we get, needs to match otherwise the relationship becomes unbalanced. Unbalanced relationships generate negativity becomes unbalanced. Unbalanced relationships generate negativity while balanced relationships absorb and reduce it.

A realtionship can survive being unbalanced for a while if there is past goodwill, however, the imbalance cannot be sustained for long. Relationships break down under a sustained imbalance.

relationship vs love

Relationships are different from love. Love is unconditional while relationships have implied conditions built into them. When we do something for others out of love, we do not expect anything in return. On the other hand, relationships have expectations built around them and if those expectations are not met, the relationship breaks down.

Confusion between love and relationship in often the reason for the breakdown of romantic relationships. When we say that we are in love, we give up control because we imply that we'll keep doing things even if our partner does not reciprocate. When in love, we ignore the imbalance till it gets too much and the relationship breaks away.

perfect relationships

A perfect relationship is one that does not generate negativity and requires no effort on our part to sustain. Only those who know the art of engaging in interactions without generating negativity can have perfect relationships.

relationship with others

When we realize that the circumstances that conditioned others to behave the way they did were out of their control, we stop holding them accountable for things they did or should have done.

Negative emtions directed towards others such as resentment, blame, anger, and hate disappear. We become more patient and tolerant towards others and our relationships then flourish.

relationship with self

When we realize that the circumstances that conditioned us to behave the way we did were out of our control, we stop blaming ourselves for things we did or should have done.

Negative feelings directed inward, such as regret, guilt and shame disappear and our relationship with our own self then flourishes.

feelings

Emotions are driven by the circumstances of our life. When the circumstances that created our emotions change, our emotions change as well, however, the old emotions continue to be felt as feelings.

A feeling is a residual emotion that is no longer supported by current circumstances. It is similar to a residual ghost image of the previous scene that we sometimes see on bad television sets.

Feelings continually degrade in intensity and eventually disappear. Repeating emotions reinforce existing feelings and increase their intensify. The strengthened feeling now takes longer to disappear.

stress

Stress gives us a temporary performance boost when we need to deal with urgent situations by putting our systems in an overdrive mode. This boost comes at a cost.

While short term stress has a minimal cost, chronic stress wears out the body and disrupts the normal functioning of emotions and feelings.

worry & anxiety

Worry is the unpleasant feeling brought about by the fear or apprehension of negative outcomes.

Worry begins as occasional, short term episodes that subsequently increase in duration and intensity progressing from worry to anxiety and then panic.

depression

Depression is a persistent emotional pain associated with feelings of sadness, hopelessness, worthlessness, or guilt. Depression destroys our ability to feel pleasure leading to loss of interest in day-to-day activities and isolation.

Depression is self-reinforcing. Once it gets a hold of you, it does not let go.

birth

We were born when the male sperm fused with the female egg to form the first cell of our body, the Zygote. The sperm and egg cells were individually alive before they fused. The resulting zygote was alive as well. The zygote then divided into the various cells of our body, each of which was individually alive.

Birth does not create Life, nor does Death destroy it. Life exists before we are born and will continue long after we die

death
